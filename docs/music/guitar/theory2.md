# Strumming & Picking

## Strumming Patterns

Common notation: **D** = down, **U** = up, **X** = muted/dead strum, **-** = pause

| Pattern     | Timing      | Example Songs                    |
|-------------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| D-DUX-DU    | 1&2&3&4&    | Somewhere Over the Rainbow       |
| D-XU-UX-    | 1&2&3&4&    | I See Fire                       |

### Syncopation

Emphasize the off-beats to create a groovy, dynamic feel.

- Example pattern: Down → Down-Up → (Pause) → Up → Down-Up

### Palm Muting

Lightly rest the palm of your strumming hand on the strings near the bridge to mute them slightly. Adds a percussive "chugging" effect — common in rock and pop.

### Percussive Strumming

Incorporate muted strums or slaps to create a rhythmic, drum-like sound.

- Example: Down → Slap → Down-Up → Slap

### Swing Feel

Strum with uneven timing, emphasizing the first part of each beat (triplet feel). Gives a jazzy or bluesy touch.

### Hybrid Strumming

Combine strumming with fingerpicking — pluck specific strings during a strum for added texture.

## Picking Techniques

### Alternate Picking

Alternate between downstrokes and upstrokes for speed and efficiency. Essential for fast solos and scales.

### Economy Picking

Combine alternate picking with directional picking to minimize pick movement. When crossing strings in the same direction, use a single sweep motion instead of reversing.

### Sweep Picking

Use a single fluid motion to "sweep" across strings while fretting arpeggios. The pick glides in one direction while each note rings individually.

### Flat Picking

Using a pick to play melodic runs cleanly. A classic technique in bluegrass and country. Practice the **G Run** as a foundational exercise.

### Hybrid Picking

Use a pick and your fingers simultaneously to pluck multiple strings. Popular in country and fingerstyle guitar.

### String Skipping

Skip strings while picking to create wide intervals. Often used in shred guitar solos for dramatic, angular phrasing.
