# Music

### Preface

This guide serves as a personal reference notebook for my journey into music and guitar. It's useful as a starting point for beginners and a reference for more experienced players - though it barely scratches the surface of the full depth of music theory and technique.

If you can master the basics here, you'll be ahead of most self-taught guitarists. *To err is human* - if you spot a mistake, report it to the repository.

PS: I do love to talk about music, so you might find a lot of content where I'm talking to myself :)

## Not Yet Learned

### Theory

- Reading sheet music and tablature (sight reading)
- Key signatures (sharps, flats, key detection)
- Time signatures (compound time, odd meters)
- Harmonic functions (tonic, dominant, subdominant groups)
- Secondary dominants
- Tritone substitutions
- Modulation and key changes
- Reharmonization
- Negative harmony
- Nashville Number System

### Technique

- Barre chords (F major shape, B minor shape)
- Fingerstyle guitar (Travis picking, arpeggios, thumb independence)
- Classical fingerstyle
- Legato techniques (hammer-ons, pull-offs, slides)
- Vibrato and bends
- Tapping
- Two-hand tapping
- Sweep picking (arpeggios)
- String skipping runs
- Hybrid picking in depth

### Scales & Soloing

- Bebop scales
- Whole tone scale
- Diminished (octatonic) scale
- Exotic scales (Gypsy, Hungarian, Japanese)
- Three-notes-per-string patterns
- Scale-chord relationships
- Target note soloing

### Chords (Advanced)

- Barre chord variations
- Jazz voicings
- Drop 2 and Drop 3 voicings
- Shell voicings
- Chord melody playing
- Quartal harmony

### Ear Training

- Interval recognition
- Chord quality identification
- Playing by ear
- Transcribing songs

### Other

- Guitar maintenance and setup
- Intonation and action adjustment
- Effects and pedals (overdrive, delay, reverb, chorus)
- Recording at home (DAW basics, microphone placement)
- Amplifier settings and tone shaping
- Sight reading for guitar
- Music history and genre context
